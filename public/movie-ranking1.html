My top 5 movies of all time.

Lone Survivor

Lone Survivor (2013), directed by Peter Berg, is a war drama based on the memoir of Navy SEAL Marcus Luttrell. The film recounts the harrowing Operation Red Wings mission in Afghanistan, where a four-man SEAL team is tasked with capturing a Taliban leader. After encountering local villagers, the team faces a moral dilemma that ultimately exposes their position to enemy fighters. What follows is a brutal and relentless firefight in the mountains, showcasing the soldiers’ courage, loyalty, and sacrifice as they fight against overwhelming odds.

The film emphasizes themes of brotherhood, duty, and the human cost of war. While three of the SEALs are killed in combat, Luttrell survives with severe injuries and is sheltered by Afghan villagers who risk their lives to protect him until U.S. forces arrive. Known for its visceral realism and intense action sequences, Lone Survivor pays tribute to the fallen soldiers and highlights the complexities of modern warfare, blending raw combat with moments of humanity and moral reflection.

The Walking Dead

The Walking Dead is a long-running post-apocalyptic horror drama series that premiered in 2010, based on the comic books by Robert Kirkman. It follows survivors navigating a world overrun by zombies—called “walkers”—while also dealing with the collapse of society and the dangers posed by other human groups. The central character at the start is Rick Grimes, a former sheriff, who awakens from a coma to find the world changed forever.

Over its 11 seasons, the show explores themes of survival, morality, leadership, and the blurred lines between humanity and savagery. It became a cultural phenomenon, spawning spin-offs like Fear the Walking Dead, The Walking Dead: World Beyond, and newer series focusing on fan-favorite characters such as Daryl Dixon, Negan, and Maggie. Its mix of intense action, emotional storytelling, and moral dilemmas helped redefine the zombie genre for television.

Olympus has Fallen

Olympus Has Fallen (2013), directed by Antoine Fuqua, is a high-octane action thriller centered on a terrorist attack against the White House. Gerard Butler stars as Mike Banning, a former Secret Service agent who finds himself trapped inside the building after heavily armed North Korean militants seize control, taking the President hostage. With Washington under siege, Banning uses his insider knowledge and combat skills to fight through the invaders, protect the President, and prevent a catastrophic national crisis.

The film blends suspense, explosive action sequences, and patriotic themes, earning comparisons to classic “Die Hard”-style thrillers. It launched a trilogy, followed by London Has Fallen (2016) and Angel Has Fallen (2019), each continuing Banning’s story as he faces new global threats. Known for its intense pacing and gritty tone, Olympus Has Fallen stands out as one of the more successful modern entries in the political action genre.
